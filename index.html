Disappearance of the historic Clare Market District in London

Description

This map to show the location of the historic Clare Market District in London, England beneath the city's modern highways and newer construction. Clare Market was an open air market and surrounding residential neighborhood established in the 1600s by the second Earl of Clare in central London. The area was one of the few that survived the Great Fire of London (1666) so many of it's structures dated back to Elizabethan times. In the early 1900s, Clare Market had become a slum with poor living conditions and many derelict buildings. The area was demolished and replaced with part of the Kingsway highway and new construction which included an extension of the London School of Economics campus.
